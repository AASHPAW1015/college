About the Album

The Dark Side of the Moon is the eighth studio album by the English rock band Pink Floyd, released on March 1, 1973. It was primarily developed during live performances, with the band premiering an early version of the record several months before recording began.

The album explores themes such as conflict, greed, time, death, and mental illness, drawing inspiration from the band's own stressful lifestyle and the mental health struggles of former member Syd Barrett.

A Lasting Legacy

The album is widely regarded as one of the greatest and most influential albums of all time. It is one of the best-selling records in history, with estimated sales of over 45 million copies worldwide. It sat on the Billboard 200 charts for a record-breaking 741 weeks (over 14 years) from 1973 to 1988.

Its iconic cover art, designed by Storm Thorgerson, depicts a prism dispersing light into color and represents the band's stage lighting, the record's lyrical themes, and keyboardist Richard Wright's request for a "simple and bold" design.

Fun Facts / Trivia

  • The album was recorded at Abbey Road Studios in London.

  • Alan Parsons served as the audio engineer for the album.

  • The voices heard throughout the album are answers to interview questions given to people working at Abbey Road, including the doorman.

  • Paul McCartney was interviewed but his contributions were not included in the final cut.
